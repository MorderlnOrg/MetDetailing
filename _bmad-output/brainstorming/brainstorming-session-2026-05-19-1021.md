# Brainstorming Session Results

**Facilitator:** Fnecula
**Date:** 2026-05-19

## Session Overview

**Topic:** Simple presentation website for a car detailing service using the provided CUSTOM Detailing Auto Mobil logo.

**Goals:** Brainstorm the full shape of the site: page structure, design direction, service list, copywriting, and useful static features that do not require persistence or a database.

### Context Guidance

The site should work as a lightweight presentation and contact funnel. Contact methods to feature: phone, WhatsApp, TikTok, Instagram, and email.

### Session Setup

The site should stay simple and practical, with the service offering and contact options easy to find. Since the business is local and service-based, ideas should prioritize trust, clarity, fast mobile contact, and a polished look that matches the existing black-and-white logo.

## Technique Selection

**Approach:** Progressive Technique Flow
**Journey Design:** Systematic development from exploration to action.

**Progressive Techniques:**

- **Phase 1 - Exploration:** What If Scenarios for maximum idea generation across structure, visuals, copy, services, and contact flow.
- **Phase 2 - Pattern Recognition:** Mind Mapping for grouping ideas into clear site sections and priorities.
- **Phase 3 - Development:** SCAMPER Method for refining the strongest concepts into a sharper site direction.
- **Phase 4 - Action Planning:** Constraint Mapping for turning the concept into an implementation-ready static website plan.

**Journey Rationale:** The site needs to feel polished and trustworthy without becoming complex. This flow starts wide enough to avoid a generic brochure site, then narrows toward a practical build that can stay static, fast, and contact-focused.

## Phase 1 - What If Scenarios

### Prompt: What if the visitor gives the site only 10 seconds before deciding whether to contact?

**User Insight:** The visitor should feel immediate impact and fast legitimacy. The site should communicate professionalism and show proof of good work, especially through before-and-after photos.

**[Trust Impact #1]: Proof-First Hero**
_Concept_: The first screen uses the logo, a sharp professional promise, and a before/after detailing result as the main visual proof. The visitor immediately sees both brand and result before reading much.
_Novelty_: Instead of a generic car image, the hero is built around actual transformation proof.

**[Trust Impact #2]: Instant Contact Strip**
_Concept_: Phone, WhatsApp, Instagram, TikTok, and email appear as clear icon actions near the top, with WhatsApp and phone prioritized. On mobile, the primary contact actions stay reachable.
_Novelty_: The site behaves less like a brochure and more like a quick contact tool.

**[Trust Impact #3]: Professional Promise Line**
_Concept_: A short line explains the value: careful mobile auto detailing, interior/exterior cleaning, and visible results. This avoids vague phrases and tells the visitor exactly what is offered.
_Novelty_: The copy establishes legitimacy in one sentence instead of relying on decorative design.

**[Trust Impact #4]: Before/After Gallery Preview**
_Concept_: The home page includes a small grid or slider of before/after images with labels like Interior, Exterior, Upholstery, Headlights, or Full Detail. Each proof item points to a real service.
_Novelty_: The service list is tied directly to visible outcomes.

**[Trust Impact #5]: Trust Micro-Badges**
_Concept_: Add small badges such as Mobile Service, Interior & Exterior, Paint-Safe Products, Appointment-Based, and Local Service Area. These are not fake awards, just fast facts.
_Novelty_: Builds credibility without needing reviews, accounts, or a database.

### Implementation Decision

The user confirmed that all initial ideas are strong and should be implemented. The site implementation should include:

- Proof-first hero with brand, promise, and before/after transformation.
- Contact-first actions for phone and WhatsApp, plus Instagram, TikTok, and email.
- Trust micro-badges.
- Service cards tied to visible results.
- Before/after gallery section.
- Simple three-step process: send photos, receive estimate, schedule the work.
- Static implementation with no database or persistence.

## Booking Feature Continuation

The user requested online booking functionality and chose the recommended lightweight approach.

**Booking Direction:** Implement a static booking request form that collects customer details and opens WhatsApp with a prefilled message. This keeps the site public, simple, and database-free while still giving visitors an online booking experience.

**Fields to collect:**

- Name
- Phone
- Desired service
- Car model/details
- Preferred date
- Preferred time interval
- Area/locality
- Optional notes

**Booking Behavior:** The form does not persist data. On submit, it validates required fields client-side and redirects to WhatsApp with a structured booking request message.
